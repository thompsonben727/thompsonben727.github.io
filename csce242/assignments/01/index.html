Bowling as a Sport

When most people think of bowling, they think of it as a fun, casual hobby and not as a sport. They would be surprised to find out just how much there is to bowling, not just throwing the ball down the lane and hoping to hit as many pins as they can. Where the sport may not be as physically taxing as other sports like basketball or football, it still has just as much engagement and competition. Below is a link that goes into much more of the things that make bowling a sport.

Why Bowling?

  • Improved Physical Health
  • Improved Mental Health
  • Improved Emotional Health

One of the major differences when bowling casually versus professionally is how the lane behaves. To make it more challenging, different patterns of oil are laid on the lane by a machine that makes the ball behave in many ways based on how you throw it. Generally, it only applies if you curve the ball when throwing it, which is why it isn't very important for a casual bowler to worry about the oil since most throw it straight. There are a lot of different patterns that are used in different leagues, tournaments, and other types of competitive play.
